    Q1.3 What do Market Cap, ROA, Relation, Relation Value mean?

    Market Cap: Market capitalization is the market value of a publicly traded company's outstanding shares. Market capitalization is equal to the share price multiplied by the number of shares outstanding.

    Return on Assets (ROA): A profitability ratio that provides how much profit a company is able to generate from its assets.

    Relation: Relation shows the cost type of two companies. There are 3 types of relations: COGS, SG&A and CAPEX. COGS: Cost of goods sold is the expense most directly involved in creating revenue. It represents the costs of producing or purchasing the goods or services sold by the company. SG&A: Selling, general & administrative expense. This category includes marketing, salaries, utility bills, technology expenses and other general costs associated with running a business. CAPEX: Capital expenditures are funds used by a company to acquire, upgrade, and maintain physical assets such as property, buildings, an industrial plant, technology, or equipment.

    Relationship Value: Amount involved in the relationship adjusted to quarterly-equivalent value.

    Q1.4 How do you divide the countries to different regions? Why do I see APAC and China at the same time?
    In order to analyze the effect of China-US trade war on the supply chain, China(mainland), Hong Kong and USA are classified as special regions in this network. That is, China(mainland) and Hong Kong are not included in APAC region, and the United States is not included in North America region.
    Q1.5 What do the color and size of a node mean? What does the thickness of a link mean?
    The color of a node represents a company’s role in the network, manufacturer, tier 1 supplier or tier 2 supplier.
    The size of a node represents a company’s market capital.
    The thickness of a link represents the relationship value of two companies.
    Q1.6 What is the difference between function Search and Show Network?

    Search: Highlight target company's network in the whole network.

    Show Network: Only show the companies in target company’s network.

    Q1.7 What is the difference between Hub Score, Authority Score, Degree Centrality, Closeness Centrality and Betweeness Centrality?

    Hub Score: Indicates the most important supplier

    Authority Score: Indicates the most important central company

    Degree Centrality: The use of the number of connected nodes

    Closeness Centrality: Nodes having shortest distance to the others are more important

    Betweeness Centrality: Number of times of a node exists in other shortest path

    Trade War Effect Analysis
    Q2.1 What are Borrow Need, Risk Score and FX Need?
    These 3 scores represents the business opportunities and risks of the respective companies within the network. The formula are as below:

    Borrow Need: Abnormal Change of Inventories – Abnormal Change of cash

    Risk Score: Abnormal Change of D/E ratio

    FX Need: Number of Foreign Suppliers

    All the scores are standardized by min-max transformation which scaled from 0 to 1. Hence, the score here is relative but not absolute.

    The rationale behind:

    Borrow Need: Increase in inventory and/or decrease in cash decrease operation efficiency

    Risk Score: Increase in D/E ratio decrease financial flexibility

    FX Need: Increase in foreign suppliers increase FX opportunities

    Q2.2 What does Abnormal Change mean?
    The abnormal changes equal to the actual performances divided by the expected performances minus 1. In order to get the estimated performances, we utilize three-factor model to calculate the expected financial performances as if there is no trade war.

    This methodology is adopted from event study. An event study is a statistical method to assess the impact of an event on the value of a firm. For example, the announcement of a merger between two business entities can be analyzed to see whether investors believe the merger will create or destroy value. The basic idea is to find the abnormal return attributable to the event being studied by adjusting for the return that stems from the price fluctuation of the market as a whole. The event study was invented by Fama, Fisher, Jensen, and Roll (1969).

    Q2.4 What do the colors of the nodes mean on network and scatter plot?

    On the network graph:
    The color represents the degree of the score. The lower the score, the color is more yellowish. The higher the degree, the color is be more reddish. 3 scores shared the same scale.

    On the scatter plot:
    The color represents the degree of the HexaNet score. The lower the score, the color is more yellowish. The higher the degree, the color is be more purple.
    HexaNet Score is an aggregated score using Euclidean metric. Here is the formula:
    $$HexaNet\ Score = \sqrt{Borrow\ Need^2+FX\ Need^2+(1-Risk)^2} $$

    Q2.5 What is Industry Landscape?
    Industry landscape contains the financial metrics abnormal changes on the country aggregate level. Besides that, it contains how financial abnormal changes correlate with their proportion of US or Chinese supplier. There are 2 degree measurements.

    Firstly, it is US/CN degree and its applied to manufacturers only. This measures how much portion of Chinese suppliers is for the US manufacturers and how much portion of US supplier is for the Chinese manufacturers. The proportion has accounted the second-tier suppliers already.

    Secondly, it is RC degree and its applied to first-tier supplier only. This measures the portion of Chinese supplier and customers for the US suppliers. Same logic applies to Chinese suppliers. This degree balanced the revenue and cost effect by COGS ratio.
